### **System Design Recommendation: Advanced Hierarchical Policy Retrieval and Agentic Synthesis Framework**

##### **1\. Strategic Vision for Policy-Centric RAG Architectures**

The implementation of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) over structured policy corpora necessitates a departure from standard vector-based architectures toward hierarchical, agent-driven frameworks. Policy documents, characterized by nested logical trees and strict conditional dependencies, present unique challenges that traditional flat semantic search fails to resolve. This failure is significantly exacerbated when systems rely on compressed topic-label phrases rather than high-dimensional embeddings, which lack the richness required to differentiate between nuanced regulatory clauses. In such an environment, the retrieval stage must be transformed from a mathematical similarity matching process into a cognitive navigation task where a Large Language Model (LLM) traverses a document outline to identify relevant logical branches.Traditional similarity matching fails to capture the strict structural logic of policy documents, leading to three primary systemic failures:

* **Vocabulary Gaps:**  The discrepancy between a user’s colloquial language and the formal jargon found in official documentation, which embeddings alone often fail to bridge.  
* **Context Dilution:**  The loss of relevant surrounding information when documents are treated as flat, isolated chunks rather than parts of a functional hierarchy.  
* **Erosion of Conditional Logic:**  The tendency of models to overlook critical modifiers such as "if," "unless," or "provided that," which define the legal validity of a policy.Establishing robust query understanding is the necessary first layer of the pipeline to mitigate these risks before any retrieval operations commence.

##### **2\. Intelligent Query Transformation and Intent Detection**

The initial stage of a high-fidelity RAG pipeline is the transformation of raw user input into a structured intent profile. In policy environments, user queries are frequently underspecified or suffer from linguistic misalignment with formal documentation. Implementing intent detection as a diagnostic process is essential for reducing "almost-relevant" retrieval traps, where the system returns documents related to the general topic but lacking the specific facts required for an accurate answer.To handle ambiguity, we employ  **Entropy-Based Ambiguity Detection** . A specialized Router-LLM assesses the sufficiency of the input; if the model determines that the entropy—the potential for multiple conflicting answers—is too high, it triggers a clarification loop. Rather than proceeding with a speculative search, the system pauses to ask the user for missing context, such as jurisdiction or employee grade, ensuring the retrieval is grounded in specific evidentiary spans.| Decomposition Technique | Mechanism | Policy-Specific Use Case | Impact on Latency || \------ | \------ | \------ | \------ || **Self-Ask** | Model generates and answers its own sub-questions | Cross-referencing maternity leave with tenure appendices | Moderate (Sequential) || **Least-to-Most** | Solves sub-problems in order of increasing complexity | Determining logical dependency chains in eligibility | High (Iterative) || **Plan-and-Solve** | Generates an upfront plan before executing retrieval | Multi-source synthesis across different benefit guides | Moderate (Linear) || **Chain-of-Thought** | Generates intermediate reasoning steps | Evaluating complex conditional logic within a single clause | Low to Moderate |  
Choosing between "Inferring Intent" and "Clarifying Questions" is a high-risk decision. In policy RAG, inference is dangerous; missing a single condition (e.g., tenure status) invalidates the result. To manage this risk, we utilize system messages that anchor the model’s behavior in a specific persona—such as an insurance adjuster or expert travel assistant—to strictly define risk tolerance and ensure the system admits ignorance when confidence is low.These structured intents are subsequently mapped to the document index through grounded reformulation.

##### **3\. Taxonomy-Grounded Query Reformulation**

A pervasive vocabulary gap exists between natural user language and formal policy jargon. To bridge this, the system must perform reformulation that is strictly grounded in the internal taxonomy of the document corpus.While "Semantic Expansion" suggests synonyms through generic embeddings, it often introduces semantic noise by suggesting terms that do not exist in the specialized vocabulary. In contrast,  **Taxonomy Alignment**  (e.g., TaxoIndex) maps colloquialisms to authorized labels. This allows the system to link "getting fired" to the formal header "involuntary termination" without introducing drift.Furthermore, the  **Elastic Retrieval Mechanism (ERM)**  offers a superior approach for production environments by using correctness-gated feedback for offline index enrichment. ERM selectively attributes atomic expansion signals to the specific document keys they benefit, progressively aligning query and document distributions with zero inference-time overhead.| Expansion Method | Grounding Source | Persistence | Risk Factor | Citations || \------ | \------ | \------ | \------ | \------ || **Semantic Expansion** | Generic embeddings/LLM | Transient | Semantic drift/noise | 6 || **TaxoIndex** | Hierarchical taxonomy | Persistent (Index-side) | High manual/setup cost | 15 || **ERM** | Correctness-gated feedback | Persistent (Index-side) | Complex implementation | 6 || **StepBack** | Higher-level principles | Transient | May miss granular facts | 19 |  
Once queries are reformulated and aligned with the corpus vocabulary, they are used to navigate hierarchical document trees.

##### **4\. Hierarchical Navigation and Listwise Retrieval Logic**

In a structured policy environment, retrieval is increasingly treated as a  **listwise selection problem** . Rather than simple vector matching, an LLM navigates a hierarchical tree of section IDs and titles to decide which branches of the policy are most relevant.The  **LATTICE framework**  facilitates this through logarithmic navigation. Using a greedy, best-first traversal and score calibration, the system prevents being trapped in sub-optimal branches. Global coherence is maintained by comparing nodes against high-relevance candidates from sibling branches and previously seen leaf nodes, ensuring the search is not skewed by local terminology matches.However, LLMs can suffer from the "Lost-in-the-Middle" phenomenon in dense sibling groups. We prescribe  **Setwise Prompting**  as the solution. By using setwise heapsort or tournament filters, we reduce LLM inferences to  $O(n \+ k \\log\_c n)$ . The mathematical formulation for selection probability ensures the model directly discriminates between documents:$$P(i \\mid Q, S) \= \\frac{\\exp \\left(g\_\\theta(Q, d\_i)\\right)}{\\sum\_{j=1}^c \\exp \\left(g\_\\theta(Q, d\_j)\\right)}$$In policy search, high recall is a prerequisite for "truth"—ensuring no exception is missed—but it must be balanced against context pollution. Irrelevant data confused the generator; therefore, these results must be refined through sophisticated reranking.

##### **5\. Reranking and Contextual Assembly**

Rerankers act as filters that restore the signal-to-noise ratio before context packing. This reduces token costs and minimizes the risk of the model incorporating irrelevant "noise."While Bi-Encoders are efficient for initial broad searches,  **Cross-Encoders**  are mandatory for reranking due to deep token-to-token interactions.| Reranking Signal | Mechanism | Target Failure Mode || \------ | \------ | \------ || **Cross-Encoder Scores** | Deep semantic interaction | Near-miss results || **Metadata Facets** | Filtering by jurisdiction/date | Operationally invalid data || **Structural Relevance** | Weighting section headers | Topic mixing || **Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF)** | Aggregating multiple retrievers | Bias in a single model |  
For  **Context Assembly** , the system must use stable formatting and consistent delimiters. Context compression should be applied only when a chunk contains  **"excessive descriptive padding,"**  ensuring that query-relevant statements and fine conditional details remain intact.This assembly ensures the final synthesis preserves the logical integrity of the policy.

##### **6\. Logic Preservation and Synthesis Guardrails**

The most insidious failure in policy RAG is the "Conditional Clause Drop," where models omit terms like "unless" or "provided that." To combat this, we incorporate  **Natural Language Inference (NLI)**  post-checks.A specialized model, specifically  **cross-encoder/nli-deberta-v3-base** , must cross-reference each factual claim against the cited passage. If a claim contradicts the source or if a prohibitive condition is omitted, the response is flagged. The system follows strict "I Don't Know" logic: if confidence scores fall below 0.60, the system must abstain from speculative answering, explicitly state when information is missing or contradictory, and return the raw documentation.| Guardrail Layer | Tooling/Model | Impact on Accuracy | Latency || \------ | \------ | \------ | \------ || **Input Scoring** | Cross-Encoder/Cosine | \+30% relevance | 100-200ms || **Output NLI** | DeBERTa-v3 | Prevents contradictions | 300-500ms || **Citation Checks** | String Match/ID Check | Prevents fabrication | \<50ms || **Faithfulness Checks** | Rationale Generation | Improves trust | 200-400ms |  
Complex multi-document cross-references are then handled through agentic loops.

##### **7\. Agentic RAG and Multi-Hop Reasoning Trajectories**

Static pipelines fail on cross-references (e.g., "See Section 5.4").  **Agentic RAG**  transforms retrieval into an iterative reasoning loop where the agent decomposes queries, fetches context, and determines if a "second hop" is necessary.We differentiate between  **Ingestion Enrichment**  (pre-resolving references during extraction) and  **Query-Time Recursion**  (detecting references dynamically). Query-time recursion offers flexibility for deep logical chains but increases token costs. To prevent infinite loops, we define formal termination conditions:  **Terminate if**  **$s\_t \= 1**$  **and**  **$t \\ge T\_{min}**$  **, or if**  **$t \= T\_{max}**$  **.**Upon termination, the system must expose an  **"evidence matrix"**  and  **"assumption table"**  to ensure transparency in the reasoning trajectory.

##### **8\. Evaluation Framework and Operational Roadmap**

Retrieval quality is the highest-leverage component of the RAG triad. Evaluation centers on  **Context Relevance** ,  **Faithfulness** , and  **Answer Relevance** .To detect "Silent Failures," we implement advanced diagnostics:  **Answer-Conditioned Recall**  (checking if retrieved documents contain all facts used in the answer),  **Ablation Testing**  (removing top chunks to observe answer drift), and  **Attribution Scoring**  (measuring token dependence on specific chunks).| RAG Quality Evaluation Dashboard | Key Metric | Diagnostic Target || \------ | \------ | \------ || **Retrieval** | Recall@k / MRR | Missing facts / Ranking quality || **Generation** | Correctness / Faithfulness | Factual match / Hallucination || **Hybrid** | Answer-Conditioned Recall | Synthesis on broken evidence || **Process** | Evidence Sufficiency | Coverage collapse |  
Operational costs for agentic calls range from $0.02 to $0.31 per query. A versioned, observable hierarchical index is required to prevent semantic drift. The strategic roadmap necessitates a transition to a  **modular RAG approach** , moving from naive patterns to authoritative, conditional-aware reasoning trajectories.
