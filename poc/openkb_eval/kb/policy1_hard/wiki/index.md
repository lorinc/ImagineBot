## Concepts
- [[concepts/children-missing-education]] — Children Missing Education refers to pupils of compulsory school age who are not registered at a school and not receiving suitable education otherwise.
- [[concepts/sexual-exploitation-of-children]] — Sexual exploitation involves adults taking advantage of children's vulnerabilities, often through manipulation, gifts, or substances.
- [[concepts/child-welfare-education]] — Child Welfare Education refers to teaching children about safety, risks, consent, and how to protect themselves from various forms of harm.
- [[concepts/types-of-child-abuse]] — Types of child abuse encompass physical, emotional, sexual maltreatment, and neglect, each requiring vigilant identification and reporting.
- [[concepts/working-together-to-safeguard-children]] — Working Together to Safeguard Children is statutory guidance in the UK outlining expectations for agencies to safeguard and promote child welfare.
- [[concepts/keeping-children-safe-in-education]] — Statutory guidance providing schools with directives on safeguarding and promoting the welfare of children.
- [[concepts/designated-safeguarding-lead]] — The Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) is a senior school staff member responsible for leading child protection and safeguarding efforts.
- [[concepts/data-protection-and-confidentiality]] — Data Protection and Confidentiality define the principles and procedures for safeguarding sensitive information within a school environment.
- [[concepts/child-protection-procedures]] — Steps and guidelines for recognizing, reporting, and responding to concerns about child abuse or neglect within a school setting.
- [[concepts/child-safeguarding]] — Child Safeguarding encompasses protecting children from harm, preventing maltreatment, ensuring their welfare, and promoting positive development.
