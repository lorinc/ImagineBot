# Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)

The [[Designated Safeguarding Lead]] (DSL) is a crucial role within a school, holding primary responsibility for [[child protection]] and [[safeguarding]] matters. This individual is a senior member of the school's leadership team, possessing the necessary authority and status to effectively carry out their duties, including committing resources and directing other staff.

## Key Responsibilities and Duties (from [[summaries/policy1_hard]])
Based on the school's Child Protection Policy (policy1_hard), the DSL's responsibilities are extensive and include:

*   **Lead Responsibility**: Taking primary, non-delegable responsibility for safeguarding and child protection within the school, though specific activities may be delegated to appropriately trained deputies.
*   **Support and Expertise**: Acting as a key source of support and expertise for the entire school community on safeguarding issues.
*   **Child-Centered Approach**: Fostering a culture where children feel listened to and their wishes and feelings are considered.
*   **Addressing Specific Needs**: Being alert to the particular needs of vulnerable children, including those in need, those with special educational needs (SEN), looked-after children, and young carers.
*   **Record Keeping**: Maintaining detailed, secure, and flagged written records of all concerns, kept separate from the pupil's general file.
*   **Referrals**: Referring cases of suspected abuse to Children's Social Care or the Police as appropriate.
*   **Absence Monitoring**: Notifying Children's Social Care if a child with a 'Child protection plan' is absent for more than three days without explanation.
*   **Information Transfer**: Ensuring that all child protection records are securely transferred to a pupil's new school upon leaving and obtaining confirmation of receipt. If the child is subject to an open case, the social worker is also informed.
*   **Child Protection Plans**: Coordinating the school's contribution to child protection plans, including active participation in core group meetings.
*   **Staff Training and Policy Adherence**: Ensuring all staff read and sign to confirm understanding of the child protection, safeguarding, and Staff Behaviour Policy (Code of Conduct).
*   **Policy Review**: Regularly reviewing and updating the child protection and safeguarding policy and procedures annually, involving the whole school community.
*   **Training Records**: Keeping a record of staff attendance at child protection training.
*   **Public Awareness**: Making the child protection and safeguarding policy publicly available (e.g., on the school's website).
*   **Parental Awareness**: Ensuring parents are aware of the school's safeguarding role and that referrals about suspected abuse and neglect may be made.

## Qualifications and Training
The DSL must be appropriately trained, with refresher training required every two years to ensure their skills and expertise remain current. Deputy DSLs must also be trained to the same level.

## Legal Framework and Guidance
The role and responsibilities of the DSL are underpinned by statutory guidance such as *[[Keeping Children Safe in Education]]* and *[[Working Together to Safeguard Children]]*, which outline the legislative requirements and expectations for schools in safeguarding and promoting children's welfare.

## Importance
The DSL plays a pivotal role in ensuring that a school effectively identifies, addresses, and prevents harm to children, fostering a safe and supportive environment where pupils can learn and thrive without fear of abuse or [[neglect]].
