# Child Welfare Education

Child Welfare Education encompasses the pedagogical efforts within a school setting to equip children with the knowledge, understanding, and skills necessary to protect themselves and understand issues related to their safety and well-being. This proactive approach aims to empower children to recognize risks, manage dangerous situations, and seek help when needed.

From `[[summaries/policy1_hard]]`, the school integrates [[Child Welfare Education]] as a crucial component of its broader [[Child protection]] and [[safeguarding]] responsibilities. This is provided through various teaching and learning opportunities, aligning with the requirement in *[[Keeping Children Safe in Education]]* that governing bodies ensure children are taught about safeguarding, including online safety, as part of a 'broad and balanced curriculum'.

Key aspects of Child Welfare Education highlighted in the policy include:

*   **Risk Management:** Children are taught to understand and manage risks through personal, social, health, and economic (PSHE) education and sex and relationships lessons. The approach is designed to foster sensible behaviour rather than fear, encouraging children to think about risks and how to reduce or manage them.
*   **[[Online safety]] and [[Cyberbullying]]:** Regular education is provided on online safety, the risks of sharing content and images online, and strategies for tackling all forms of [[bullying]], including cyberbullying.
*   **Consent and Healthy Relationships:** Discussions about risk explicitly include issues of consent and fostering healthy relationships, which are vital for preventing harm.
*   **[[Sexting]] and [[Sexual Exploitation of Children]]:** Children are educated about the risks and issues associated with 'sexting' and the coercive nature of relationships involved in sexual exploitation. Staff are trained to be vigilant for signs related to these behaviours, recognizing that children may not always perceive themselves as victims initially.
*   **Empowerment and Communication:** The school promotes an ethos of respect, encouraging pupils to speak to trusted staff members about any worries they may have. The document mentions the 'Protective Behaviours curriculum', which teaches children about confidentiality and the concept of safe and unsafe secrets, helping them understand why staff cannot keep certain information confidential but will pass it on only on a 'need-to-know' basis.
*   **Fundamental Values:** The curriculum also covers fundamental values that contribute to a safe and respectful environment.

By embedding these topics into the curriculum and school life, the goal is to build children's confidence and provide them with a range of contacts and strategies to ensure their own protection and understand the importance of protecting others, as outlined in the 'Standards for Effective Child Protection Practice in Schools' in the appendix of `[[summaries/policy1_hard]]`.
