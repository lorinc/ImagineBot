# Working Together to Safeguard Children

**Working Together to Safeguard Children** refers to key statutory guidance issued by the Department for Education (DfE) in the United Kingdom. This guidance sets out the legislative requirements and expectations on individual services, including schools, to safeguard and promote the welfare of children.

## Key Aspects from `[[summaries/policy1_hard]]`:

In the context of `[[summaries/policy1_hard]]` (Child Protection Policy), *Working Together to Safeguard Children* is cited as a foundational document for the school's safeguarding arrangements. Specifically:

*   **Statutory Guidance**: The policy states that the *Working Together to Safeguard Children* (DfE 2015/2024) guidance covers the legislative requirements and expectations for services, including schools, regarding [[Safeguarding Children]] and promoting their welfare.
*   **Definitions of Abuse**: The definitions of the four categories of abuse (physical abuse, emotional abuse, sexual abuse, and neglect) used within the school's child protection procedures are explicitly taken from *Working Together to Safeguard Children* (HM Government, 2015).
*   **Reference Document**: The guidance is listed as a primary [[Reference Documents]] informing the school's policies and practices.

This guidance is crucial for fostering an [[Inter-agency Approach]] to child protection, ensuring that different professionals and organizations collaborate effectively to protect children from harm.
