# Children Missing Education

Children Missing Education (CME) refers to children of compulsory school age who are not registered at a school and are not receiving a suitable education in any other way. This concept is a critical aspect of [[safeguarding]] and [[child protection]] within educational settings, as outlined in the school's [[summaries/policy1_hard]].

## Importance and Risks

The school recognizes that all children are entitled to an efficient, full-time education suitable to their age, ability, aptitude, and any special educational needs. Children who are missing education are considered to be at significant risk. These risks include:

*   Underachieving academically.
*   Becoming victims of harm, exploitation, or radicalization.
*   Becoming NEET (not in education, employment, or training) later in life.

Effective information sharing among parents, schools, and local authorities is crucial to ensure all compulsory school-age children are safe and receiving suitable education.

## School Procedures

As detailed in [[summaries/policy1_hard]] (Section 1.8), the school implements specific procedures to address children missing education:

1.  **Monitoring Attendance**: [[Attendance]], absence, and exclusions are closely monitored. The [[Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)]] plays a key role in monitoring unauthorized absences and taking appropriate action, especially for repeated or extended periods of absence during the school day.
2.  **Referral for Unexplained Absence**: If a pupil has 10 consecutive school days of unexplained absence, and all reasonable steps have been taken by the school to establish their whereabouts without success, the school makes an immediate referral to the appropriate local social services.
3.  **Reasonable Steps**: The "reasonable steps" taken by the school to locate a missing child include:
    *   Telephone calls to all known contacts.
    *   Emails to relatives.
    *   Contact with other schools where siblings might be registered.
    *   Enquiries with friends, neighbours, etc., through school contacts.
    *   Enquiries with any other service known to be involved with the pupil/family.
    *   Possible home visits, where safe to do so.
    *   All contacts and outcomes are meticulously recorded on the pupil's file.
4.  **Registration Requirements**: The school is legally required to maintain both an admission register and an attendance register, and all pupils must be placed on both.

## Related Concepts and Guidance

This policy aligns with statutory guidance such as *[[Children missing education - Statutory guidance for local authorities]]* (DfE 2024), ensuring that the school's practices are in line with national expectations for identifying and supporting children who are not receiving an education. The overall goal is to prevent children from falling through the cracks and to uphold their right to education and safety as part of broader [[safeguarding]] responsibilities.
