# Keeping Children Safe in Education

**[[Keeping Children Safe in Education]]** (KCSiE) is statutory guidance issued by the Department for Education (DfE) in the UK. It sets out the legal duties and responsibilities that schools and colleges must adhere to in order to [[safeguard]] and promote the welfare of children and young people. This guidance is fundamental to establishing effective [[Child Protection]] practices within educational settings.

### Core Purpose and Scope
As highlighted in [[summaries/policy1_hard]], KCSiE is issued under key legislative frameworks, including Section 175 of the Education Act 2002 and the Education (Independent School Standards) Regulations 2014. It applies to all types of schools – maintained, non-maintained, independent, academies, free schools, alternative provision academies, maintained nursery schools, and pupil referral units.

Schools are legally required to 'have regard' to this guidance when carrying out their duties to [[safeguard]] and promote the welfare of children. This means they must follow the guidance unless there are exceptional reasons not to, and those reasons must be justifiable.

### Key Requirements and Implications for Schools

1.  **Staff Responsibilities**: All staff members in a school are mandated to read Part One of the KCSiE guidance. This ensures a foundational understanding of their [[safeguarding]] responsibilities and the procedures for reporting concerns.

2.  **Curriculum Integration**: Governing bodies and proprietors are required to ensure that children are taught about [[safeguarding]], including online safety, through the curriculum. This includes personal, social, health, and economic (PSHE) education and sex and relationships lessons, helping children understand and manage risks, as detailed in the school's policy on [[Helping Children to Keep Themselves Safe]].

3.  **[[Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)]]**: The guidance outlines the critical role of the [[Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)]], specifying their responsibilities, authority, and the need for appropriate training. The DSL acts as the central point of contact for [[child protection]] concerns within the school and liaises with external agencies like Children's Social Care and the police.

4.  **Responding to Concerns**: KCSiE provides frameworks for how staff should respond to disclosures of abuse, how to identify [[Indicators of Abuse]], and the appropriate steps for making referrals. It emphasizes that staff must *report* concerns rather than *investigate* them.

5.  **Allegations Against Staff**: The guidance includes procedures for handling allegations of abuse made against members of staff, ensuring a clear and correct process is followed, often in conjunction with local authority designated officers (LADOs).

### Relationship with Other Guidance
[[Keeping Children Safe in Education]] works in conjunction with other key statutory guidance, most notably *[[Working Together to Safeguard Children]]*, which provides a broader framework for inter-agency cooperation to [[safeguard]] and promote the welfare of children. While *Working Together* outlines the responsibilities of various agencies, KCSiE focuses specifically on the duties and practices within educational settings.

The school's `policy1_hard` explicitly references the 2024 editions of both *[[Keeping Children Safe in Education]]* and *[[Working Together to Safeguard Children]]*, demonstrating its commitment to staying current with statutory requirements and best practices in [[Child Protection]].
