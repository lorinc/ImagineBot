# Data Protection and Confidentiality

[[Data Protection and Confidentiality]] refers to the set of policies, procedures, and legal frameworks governing the handling and safeguarding of sensitive and personal information within an organization, particularly a school. It ensures privacy, prevents unauthorized access or disclosure, and dictates how information is collected, stored, shared, and managed.

## Core Principles

As outlined in [[summaries/policy1_hard]], the school is committed to maintaining confidentiality and respecting privacy in all operations. Key principles include:

*   **Staff Adherence:** All staff members must adhere to confidentiality principles and [[Data Protection Legislation (GDPR)]] to safeguard personal and sensitive information.
*   **Need-to-Know Basis:** Information should only be shared with individuals who genuinely require it for their professional duties.
*   **Legal Compliance:** All procedures must align with legal obligations and relevant data protection laws.
*   **Parental Consent:** Consent from parents or guardians is generally sought before sharing sensitive information with external parties, unless legally required (e.g., in cases of [[Child Protection]] concerns where sharing without consent is mandated to protect the child).

## Key Practices and Procedures

The school employs several practices to ensure robust [[Data Protection and Confidentiality]]:

### Information Handling and Storage
*   **Confidential Information** includes personal details of students, parents, staff, and associated documents or records.
*   **Sensitive Information** refers to highly private data, such as health records or personal identifiers, subject to legal protection.
*   **Secure Systems:** Student data and other confidential records are stored electronically in secure school management systems (e.g., Transparent Classroom, Phidias) which are user and password protected. Physical documents containing sensitive information are kept locked in the school office.
*   **Schoolwork Records:** Children's schoolwork records contain only the child's name (not address) and are stored in classroom files and the management system, accessible to teachers but not casually to visitors. Parents can review their own child's files upon request during meetings.
*   **Special Educational Needs (SEN) Records:** Review information, professional discussions, and reports for SEN children are kept electronically with user/password protection or locked physically. Parental permission is sought before involving outside professionals.

### Access Control
*   **Restricted Access:** Access to the school database and other platforms holding sensitive information is restricted to administrative staff and other limited, authorized personnel.
*   **Staff Training:** All staff are trained on the importance of treating information confidentially and understanding the appropriate protocols for information sharing.
*   **Staff Agreements:** All staff sign an annual agreement to respect the school's [[Confidentiality Policy]].

### Digital Security
*   **Password Protection:** All computers used by staff are user and password protected.
*   **Electronic Archiving:** Registration documents with confidential details are stored electronically and password-protected. Children's records that are not transferred to their next school are archived electronically for five years.

### Exceptions and Related Considerations
*   **[[Child Protection]] Concerns:** In cases where there is a concern about a child's welfare, information sharing protocols may differ. The [[Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)]] is responsible for securely keeping detailed records of concerns, separate from general pupil files. If notifying parents could increase risk to the child, advice is sought from Children's Social Care before disclosure. This highlights the balance between confidentiality and the paramount duty to [[Safeguarding]].
*   **[[Children Missing from Education]]:** Effective information sharing between parents, schools, and local authorities is considered critical to ensuring that all children are safe and receiving suitable education, even if it involves sharing sensitive data.

## Monitoring and Review

The school's Leadership Team is responsible for biennially monitoring and reviewing the [[Confidentiality Policy]] to ensure its effectiveness and compliance with evolving legislation and best practices. Changes may be made sooner if new incidents or legislation necessitate an interim review.
