# Summary of policy1_hard

## Overview
This document outlines two critical school policies: the [[Child Protection Policy]] and the [[Confidentiality Policy]]. It details the school's responsibilities, procedures, and legal frameworks for ensuring the safety and welfare of children, alongside guidelines for handling sensitive information.

## 1. Child Protection Policy
This policy mandates all staff to report concerns about a child's welfare, including risks from [[neglect]], [[physical abuse]], [[emotional abuse]], or [[sexual abuse]], in accordance with UK and Spanish law (e.g., Children Act 1989). Staff generally discuss concerns with parents but will refer to Children's Social Care if necessary, especially if discussing with parents might increase risk.

### Key Definitions
*   **[[Safeguarding]] and promoting welfare:** Protecting children from maltreatment, preventing harm, ensuring safe care, and enabling best outcomes.
*   **[[Child Protection]]:** Processes for children identified as suffering or at risk of significant harm.
*   **Staff:** All individuals working for or on behalf of the school.
*   **Child:** Everyone under 18.
*   **Parent:** Birth parents and other parenting adults.

### Principles and Aims
The policy emphasizes the paramount importance of [[safeguarding]] children, equal rights to protection, and the link between safety and learning. All staff and volunteers are expected to share this commitment, maintain an "It could happen here" attitude, and act in the child's best interests. Aims include providing information, ensuring consistent good practice, demonstrating commitment, and contributing to the school's safeguarding portfolio.

### Legislation and Guidance
The policy is guided by Section 157 of the Education Act 2002, the Education (Independent School Standards) Regulations 2014, Teacher Standards 2012, *[[Working Together to Safeguard Children]]* (DfE 2015/2024), and *[[Keeping Children Safe in Education]]* (DFE 2016/2024). Staff are trained to recognize signs of abuse and understand reporting procedures.

### Roles and Responsibilities
*   **[[Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL)]]**: A senior staff member (Sheila Ortiz Morales, Lindsay Kerr) responsible for leading [[safeguarding]] and [[child protection]], committing resources, providing expertise, maintaining records, referring suspected abuse, and coordinating with external agencies. The DSL also ensures staff training and policy review.
*   **Deputy DSL**: Trained to the same level as the DSL, fulfilling duties in the DSL's absence.

### Specific Safeguarding Areas
*   **[[Attendance]]**: Monitored closely as full attendance is vital for well-being.
*   **[[Children Missing from Education]]**: Procedures for monitoring unexplained absences and making referrals to appropriate local social services if a child is missing for 10 consecutive school days after reasonable steps to locate them have failed.
*   **[[Helping Children to Keep Themselves Safe]]**: Taught through personal, social, health, and economic (PSHE) education and sex and relationships lessons. Topics include online safety, managing risks, tackling bullying (including cyberbullying), [[consent]], and issues like [[sexting]].
*   **[[Complaints Procedure]]**: Addresses concerns about poor practice that don't initially meet the [[child protection]] threshold, with complaints escalating to [[child protection procedures]] if warranted.

### Child Protection Procedures
1.  **Recognizing Abuse**: Understanding different types of maltreatment: [[Physical Abuse]] (e.g., hitting, fabricated illness), [[Emotional Abuse]] (e.g., persistent maltreatment causing adverse emotional effects), [[Sexual Abuse]] (forcing or enticing sexual activities, physical or non-contact), and [[Neglect]] (persistent failure to meet basic physical/psychological needs). Definitions are from *[[Working Together to Safeguard Children]]* (HM Government, 2015).
2.  **Indicators of Abuse**: Staff are trained to observe both physical (e.g., bruises, burns) and behavioral signs (e.g., changes in behavior, self-harm, withdrawal, knowledge of drugs/alcohol, sexual knowledge beyond age). Staff's role is to report concerns, not to investigate.
3.  **Impact of Abuse**: Acknowledges that abuse can lead to long-term physical, emotional, and psychological difficulties.
4.  **[[Taking Action]]**: Guidelines for staff when a pupil discloses abuse, emphasizing active listening, non-investigative questions, reassuring the child, and explaining that information must be passed on to the DSL. Notifying parents requires careful consideration; if the school believes it could increase risk to the child, advice will be sought first from Children's Social Care.

### [[Sexual Exploitation of Children]]
The school educates children on [[consent]] and the risks of sexual exploitation, which involves adults taking advantage of vulnerable children. Staff are to act on concerns, even if the child doesn't recognize themselves as a victim.

### Local Contact Telephones
A list of local emergency and support services in Spain (Paterna, Valencia) for reporting concerns (e.g., Policía Local, Servicios Sociales, Teléfono de Emergencias de Menores).

### Standards for Effective Child Protection Practice
An appendix outlines 14 standards covering school ethos, support systems, parental engagement, early help, vigilance, record-keeping, training, inter-agency liaison, curriculum integration, clear policies, anti-bullying efforts, support for SEN pupils, and handling allegations against staff.

## 2. Confidentiality Policy
This policy ensures professional behavior regarding confidentiality, respecting privacy within legal parameters and [[Data Protection Legislation (GDPR)]].

### Key Definitions
*   **[[Confidential Information]]:** Personal details of students, parents, staff, and related documents.
*   **[[Sensitive Information]]:** Private information like health records, subject to legal protection.
*   **Management System:** Secure school database platforms (Transparent Classroom, Phidias).

### Principles and Procedures
The school commits to maintaining confidentiality, with all staff adhering to data protection laws. Information is shared only on a "need-to-know" basis, with parental consent usually sought before sharing with external parties, unless legally required. Procedures include staff training, secure storage of records (physical and electronic), restricted access to management systems, and annual staff agreement to the policy.

### Staff DBS Checks
All staff undergo full enhanced police checks (DBS checks) during recruitment.

## Monitoring and Review
Both policies are subject to regular review (at least annually for [[Child Protection Policy]], biennially for [[Confidentiality Policy]]) by school leadership or the Imagine Leadership Team.
