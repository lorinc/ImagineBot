# 1. Fire and Emergency Policy

## 1.1. Scope

The safety of the children and staff at Imagine Montessori School is paramount. Therefore, the risk assessment ensures the safety of everyone. There is an emergency drill carried out every term. A risk assessment is made annually or when there is a significant change or new risk to consider.

**The Health and Safety Officers:**

*   Identify the hazard, record significant findings and assess the risks
*   Review the existing control measures
*   Where necessary, provide additional control measures
*   Carry out risk assessments

**The External Technician in Emergency Risk Assessment is responsible for:**

*   Identification of hazards
*   Detect who might be harmed and how
*   Evaluate the risks, deciding whether existing controls are adequate, or the school needs to do more
*   Record findings
*   Review and revise annually

These responsible agents collaborate to prepare an emergency plan in accordance with the risks identified. The emergency plan is validated with the Leadership Team and explained and practised with the whole Imagine Team.

## 1.2. Terminology

Fire and Emergency Policy: The policy established to ensure the safety of individuals in the building in the event of a fire or other emergency.

Risk Assessment: The process of identifying and evaluating risks to ensure a safe environment.

Health and Safety Officers: Personnel responsible for identifying hazards, implementing control measures, and overseeing safety procedures.

External Technician in Emergency Risk Assessment: An external specialist responsible for evaluating emergency risks and providing expert guidance.

Emergency Plan: A document outlining the procedures to be followed during an emergency to ensure the safety of everyone on the premises.

Fire Assembly Point: A designated area where individuals must gather after evacuating the building.

Contractor Hazard Exchange Form/Checklist: A tool used to assess and mitigate specific risks associated with contractors working on the premises.

## 1.3. Policy Statement and Principles

Imagine Montessori School is committed to ensuring the safety of all children, staff, and visitors on campus. This commitment is reflected in the implementation of policies and practices that:

1.  Ensure continuous risk assessments to identify hazards and mitigate them effectively.
2.  Promote regular drills and staff training to guarantee swift and organised responses in case of emergencies.
3.  Provide clear information and evacuation procedures to all parties involved, including families, visitors, and contractors.
4.  Maintain accessible evacuation routes and ensure emergency equipment is in optimal condition through regular inspections.

These principles are designed to ensure a safe and orderly environment during any emergency.

## 1.4. Emergency Evacuation Procedures for Families, Visitors, and Volunteers

In case any family, visitors or volunteers are on the school premises when an emergency evacuation takes place, please follow the instructions of the member of staff in charge.

## 1.5. Staff absences

Should any member of staff be absent, their duties in evacuating the children in their care from the building will be undertaken by the teacher or adult in charge of the class. Supply staff will also be required to sweep any areas they pass through for personnel as they exit the building.

## 1.6. Visitor Registration and Safety Procedures

**Visitor Registration**

All visitors and contractors must report to Reception, signing in upon arrival and signing out before leaving the premises. Once signed in, they will receive a green lanyard, which must be worn throughout their visit. During a fire evacuation, the staff member hosting the visitor is responsible for ensuring they are escorted to the designated fire assembly point.

**Contractors Safety Procedures**

For contractors, there is a distinction between recurrent and non-recurrent roles:

*   **Recurrent contractors (e.g., cleaning or kitchen staff)** have their adapted Fire and Emergency Protocol, and receive basic fire safety training to ensure they are prepared for emergencies, even outside regular school hours. They are informed of the action to be taken on hearing the fire alarm or discovering a fire, the evacuation procedures including means of escape, location of the fire assembly points and name of the person in charge of evacuation procedures, and the location of firefighting equipment and fire alarm call points in relation to the area of their work.
*   **Non-recurrent contractors** receive a brief summary of emergency procedures upon arrival, including the location of emergency exits and the fire assembly point. In the event of an evacuation, they are instructed to join the nearest evacuation team and follow their guidance to ensure a safe exit.

This approach ensures all individuals on the premises are informed and capable of responding appropriately in an emergency.

## 1.7. Evacuation routes

Evacuation routes will be kept free from obstruction and adequately and clearly marked.

## 1.8. Fire alarm test

The fire alarms are regularly tested by a certified agent.

## 1.9. Fire Fighting equipment

Firefighting equipment will be examined and tested at least once a year by a competent service engineer.

## 1.10. Monitoring and Review

The Imagine Leadership Team is responsible for monitoring and reviewing the policy biennially.

## 1.11. Reference Documents

*   Risk Assessment and Emergency Planning Guide for Educational Institutions.
*   Fire Evacuation and Prevention Regulations from the Department for Education and Safety.
*   Contractor Hazard Exchange Forms and Checklists.

# 2. Health & Safety Policy

## 2.1. Scope

Children should have the opportunity to engage in a broad variety of activities. Health and safety measures should support this goal, ensuring their well-being without hindering their participation. It is crucial for children to learn to recognise and manage risks as part of their everyday life. When assessing and managing risks, common sense should be applied, and health and safety procedures should always be proportionate to the risks associated with each activity. Staff should receive appropriate training to ensure they can protect themselves and the children, and effectively manage any potential risks.

**Responsibility of the school**

The employer is required to have a health and safety policy in place and ensure the necessary measures are implemented to enforce it. This is regulated under the Law 31/1995 on the *Prevention of Occupational Risks*. Employers are obligated to assess the risks associated with all activities, adopt measures to manage those risks, and communicate these measures to their employees. Furthermore, the organisation of preventive activities is governed by the Regulation of Prevention Services (*Royal Decree 39/1997*).

In practice, employers may delegate specific health and safety tasks to designated individuals. However, ultimate responsibility always remains with the employer, regardless of who carries out the tasks.

It is therefore essential for the employer to maintain a clear system of documentation and monitoring to ensure there is an audit trail. This should specify who is responsible for each task and confirm that these tasks are being performed appropriately.

**Responsibility of the employees**

At Imagine Montessori School, responsibility for health and safety is shared across different roles. To manage health and safety effectively, the following responsibilities are defined:

*   **Policy Definition and Oversight:** Specific areas are assigned to designated individuals, such as the Responsibility for Workplace Safety (HR Responsible) and oversight of Buildings and Facilities (Facility Manager).
*   **Implementation:** The practical application of health and safety measures is managed by the Head Teachers and the Head of Administration within their respective domains.

This collaborative structure ensures that all areas of the school’s operations maintain the highest standards of safety and compliance.

## 2.2. Terminology

Risk Assessment: The process of identifying hazards, evaluating risks, and implementing measures to control them.

First Aider: A staff member trained and certified to provide immediate medical assistance.

## 2.4. First aid

All Imagine’s staff have qualified first-aider status and are trained on an annual recurrent basis. Therefore, we have well above the required ratio of first aiders to pupils. For minor accidents, they will refer any accidents or any other medical conditions to the designated member of staff: Secretary. For medical emergencies, the person who finds the child/adult will deal with it immediately. Only trained first aiders administer first aid.

There is always more than one designated first-aider on the site of the school. All first aiders attend training courses biannually. First aid equipment is in the designated first aid cupboard, which is kept in the admin staff room and bathroom.

All accidents and other medical emergencies should be recorded appropriately in an accident report form. Parents /guardians should be notified and asked to sign the record sheet and a record kept. In the case of students using our bus service, parents will be notified by Phidias and we expect an acknowledgment. The accident report will be kept both on paper and in digital format at the Secretary.

Based on the information from accident reports, risk analyses are periodically conducted using multivariable analysis to implement mitigation actions for recurring potential risks.

If the accident is serious, the head teacher should be made aware and immediate action taken to ensure the location of the accident is still safe to use. Necessary action should be taken and where possible details recorded for an accident investigation. If members of the public are involved, names and addresses should be taken (including any witnesses).

Members of staff should be made aware of any hazards in the subjects that they teach or the activities that they supervise and should have appropriate training and access to first aid kits.

Pupils with medical conditions, such as epilepsy, asthma or severe allergies should be identified. All staff should be aware of their conditions and alert to the need for prompt action. The Secretary administers medication and is witnessed by another member of staff. A written record is kept and signed by the two members of staff. Medication is kept in the office or the refrigerator in Reception. A signed consent form is needed from the parent /guardian before prescribed medication is administered.

Staff should be aware of what levels of injury can be treated on-site and what levels automatically trigger an emergency ambulance call or visit to the hospital.

There are hygiene procedures in place in case of spillage of blood or body fluid: Employees must wear disposable gloves and aprons when cleaning any bodily fluids (spillages of blood, vomit, urine and excreta). Other persons should be kept away from the contamination until it is effectively dealt with.

The following action should be taken in the event of spillages:

*   Soak up the spillages using paper towels
*   Clean the area with hot water containing soap or detergent
*   Clean the area with a bleach or disinfectant solution - use as directed by the manufacturer.
*   'Solids’ should be carefully flushed down the toilet. Dressings, gloves, and aprons should be carefully disposed of in sealed plastic bags as clinical waste.

## 2.5. Protocol for action in case of wasp stings in students

**1. Initial assessment and safety**

*   Immediately move the student away from the area where the wasps are present to prevent further stings.
*   Check for additional wasps nearby before intervening.

**2. Assess severity**

*   Ask and observe:
    *   Single sting or multiple stings?
    *   Are you known to be allergic to insect stings?
    *   Initial symptoms:
        *   Mild: pain, redness, localised swelling.
        *   Emergency: difficulty breathing, swelling of the face/neck, widespread hives, dizziness, vomiting.

**3. Immediate action according to the situation**

**A. Mild local reaction (most common)**

*   Remove the sting if present (wasps usually do not leave one, but check).
*   Wash the area with soap and water.
*   Apply a cold compress (ice wrapped in a cloth) for 10–15 minutes to reduce swelling and pain.
*   Elevate the affected area if possible (e.g. an arm).
*   Observe the student for at least 30 minutes to rule out delayed reactions.

**B. Severe reaction (anaphylaxis) – EMERGENCY**

Warning symptoms:

*   Swelling of the lips, tongue, or throat.
*   Shortness of breath or a feeling of tightness in the chest.
*   Severe dizziness or loss of consciousness.
*   Generalised urticaria.
*   Persistent vomiting.

**Action:**

*   Call 112 immediately (state: *“possible anaphylactic reaction due to sting”*).
*   Administer self-injectable adrenaline (if reported by the family and available at the school).
*   Place the child lying down with legs elevated (if conscious).
*   Do not leave the child unattended until medical assistance arrives.

**4. Information and monitoring**

*   Notify the family immediately.
*   Record the incident (time, place, symptoms, and actions taken).
*   Monitor the student for at least 1 hour, even in mild cases, as delayed reactions may occur.

**5. Prevention**

*   Check the premises before use.
*   Inform staff to act quickly but calmly.
*   Ensure a first aid kit with instant cold packs and basic materials is available.
*   Keep a list of students with known allergies and the location of their auto-injectors.

**6. Training of staff in the use of an EpiPen**

**How to use the EpiPen:**

1.  Remove the safety cap.
2.  Hold firmly and place against the outer thigh (it can be applied over clothing).
3.  Press until you hear a click, hold for 5–10 seconds.
4.  Massage the area gently.

*   After use: call 112 and notify the family.
*   Ensure the child is taken to hospital, even if symptoms improve.
*   School Emergency Kit:
    *   Instant cold packs (for mild stings).
    *   Oral antihistamine (only if prescribed for the student).
    *   Gauze, gloves, disinfectant.
    *   List of emergency telephone numbers (112, family contacts).
    *   List of students with allergies and the location of auto-injectors (if applicable).

## 2.7. Reference Documents

The main legislation covering health and safety in educational settings in the Valencian Community includes the Decreto 253/2019, de 29 de noviembre (DOGV), which regulates the organisation and functioning of public centres providing Early Childhood and Primary Education. This decree establishes guidelines for comprehensive safety within schools, covering aspects related to infrastructure, emergency measures, and protocols for action.

The Ley 10/2014, de 29 de diciembre, de Salud de la Comunitat Valenciana, specifies that the competent authorities, in collaboration with the educational and child protection departments, will develop educational and training programmes to promote healthy habits and prevent diseases in the school environment.

The Ley 1/1994, de 28 de marzo, de Salud Escolar, also establishes actions related to health in schools, with a strong emphasis on promoting health and preventing diseases among the student population.

In addition, the Ley 31/1995 (Occupational Risk Prevention Act) and the Reglamento de los Servicios de Prevención (Regulation of Prevention Services) apply to the educational environment, ensuring that risks are identified, managed, and mitigated. Employees also have a duty to ensure their own and others' health and safety. It is rare for school staff to face prosecution under criminal law regarding accidents involving children, as responsibility is generally considered under civil law, with a standard of care similar to that of a prudent parent.

# 3. Policy of action in the event of weather alerts and emergencies

## 3.1. Objective

To guarantee the safety of students, teaching and non-teaching staff, and families in weather alert situations, by establishing an action protocol in line with the level of risk determined by the Generalitat Valenciana, AEMET and the competent authorities.

## 3.2. Alert levels and action measures

| Level   | Meaning                                                               |
| :------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Green** | There is no meteorological risk.                                      |
| **Yellow**| There is some meteorological risk for certain specific activities, but not for the population in general. |
| **Orange**| There is a significant meteorological risk. Unusual weather phenomena with a certain degree of danger for normal activities. |
| **Red**   | The meteorological risk is extreme. Unusual weather phenomena of exceptional intensity, posing a very high risk for the population. |

**GREEN LEVEL: NO RISK**

*   No special measures are taken.
*   The normal activity of the centre is maintained.

**YELLOW LEVEL: MODERATE RISK**

*   Activities at the centre:
    *   All outdoor activities (physical education, outdoor play, excursions) are suspended.
    *   A visual inspection of the premises is carried out to detect possible risks (pipes, drains, loose structures, etc.).
*   Monitoring and communication:
    *   The alert is monitored regularly through official emergency channels.
    *   Families are informed of the measures taken.

**ORANGE LEVEL: SIGNIFICANT RISK**

*   Activities at the centre:
    *   All outdoor activities (physical education, outdoor play, excursions) are suspended.
    *   A visual inspection of the premises is carried out to detect possible risks (pipes, drains, loose structures, etc.).
*   Monitoring and communication:
    *   The alert is monitored regularly through official emergency channels.
    *   Families are informed of the measures taken.
*   Transport and accessibility:
    *   Liaise with the town council or Civil Protection to assess the condition of the access roads to the centre.
    *   Early closure of the centre will be considered if conditions require it.
    *   If staff or families live in areas affected by the alert, they are urged to take extreme care when travelling and to notify the school of possible delays.

**RED LEVEL: EXTREME RISK**

*   Closure criteria:
    *   The Management may determine the total or partial closure of the educational centre depending on the severity of the alert and in accordance with the indications of the competent authorities (town council and/or Civil Protection).
*   Transport, accessibility and evacuation:
    *   Coordination with those responsible for school transport to ensure safe management.
    *   If the alert is declared during school hours, the evacuation or confinement plan due to weather risk will be evaluated and activated if necessary.
*   Monitoring and communication:
    *   Continuous contact with the town council and/or Civil Protection.
    *   In the event of preventive closure, staff and families will be informed not to attend.
    *   If the alert is declared while the centre is in operation, the necessary measures will be taken and communicated to guarantee everyone’s safety.

## 3.3. Coordination with the generalitat valenciana and local authorities

*   The instructions of the GVA and local authorities will be followed regarding any decision on the closure of the centre.
*   The emergency website of the Generalitat Valenciana ([https://www.112cv.gva.es/es/1-1-2-on-line](https://www.112cv.gva.es/es/1-1-2-on-line)), the AEMET website and the indications of Civil Protection will be consulted to adapt the necessary measures.
*   In the event of an emergency declared by the GVA at level 0, 1 or 2, the school will assess additional measures to ensure safety.

## 3.4. Suspension of classes and educational continuity

**If the Generalitat Valenciana (GVA) or the town council orders the closure:**

*   Classes must be suspended.
*   The school will inform families and staff about the suspension of activities.
*   Coordination will be carried out with school transport and auxiliary services.

**If there is no closure order, but an evident risk exists:**

*   The school may decide to close as a precautionary measure.
*   The decision must be communicated to families in advance.
*   If the alert is activated during the school day, dismissal may be brought forward or confinement applied if access to the outside is unsafe.

**If an orange or red alert is expected outside school hours:**

*   The indications of Civil Protection and the GVA will be followed to evaluate the situation.
*   If classes are suspended, families will be informed and supported regarding the possibility of offering online educational activities if circumstances allow.

## 3.5. General preventive measures

To minimise the risks associated with adverse weather conditions, the following preventive measures are carried out periodically:

*   Periodic review of the condition of generator sets.
*   Cleaning and maintenance of pipes, gutters and drains.
*   Verification of first aid kits and materials.
*   Switching off unnecessary electrical equipment during storms.

## 3.6. Query and monitoring of alerts

Alerts will be checked through the official emergency website: [https://www.112cv.gva.es/es/1-1-2-on-line](https://www.112cv.gva.es/es/1-1-2-on-line), the AEMET website, and other official sources.

## 3.7. Responsibility and coordination

*   The Head of the Emergency Plan in risk situations will be responsible for supervising the implementation of these measures.
*   Contact will be maintained with Civil Protection and the town council to adapt actions according to the evolution of the risk.

This protocol will be updated in line with current regulations and the recommendations of meteorological and civil protection authorities.

# 4. Food Policy

## 4.1. Scope

This policy applies to all students and staff at Imagine Montessori School, covering all aspects of food provision, including meals, snacks, and dietary considerations. In alignment with our Montessori philosophy, it encompasses the preparation, serving, and cultural approach to food, ensuring that all meals are nutritious and supportive of the environmental values upheld by the school.

## 4.2. Terminology

**Nutritive Food:** Food that is balanced, providing essential nutrients to support physical, cognitive, and emotional development.

**Organic Products:** Food grown without synthetic pesticides, fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms (GMO), in accordance with organic farming standards.

**Seasonal Products:** Ingredients that are grown or harvested at the peak of their natural season, ensuring freshness and minimal environmental impact.

**Km 0:** Food sourced locally, minimizing transportation costs and environmental footprint.

**Special Diets:** We follow any special diet due to health (gluten-free, specific food allergies, with a certificate from allergologists or gastroenterologists), or personal (vegetarian, vegan) reasons.

## 4.3. Policy Statement and Principles

**Nutritional Balance:** We are committed to offering three nutritious meals daily: A portion snack in the morning, lunch, and snack in the afternoon, with a focus on whole, unprocessed foods. Our meals are free of fried products, low in salt, and free from added sugars.

**Sustainability and Environment:** In line with Montessori values and our commitment to environmental stewardship, ingredients used in school meals are organic, seasonal, and locally sourced (“km 0”). This not only supports the health of our students but also contributes to global sustainability efforts.

**Inclusive Diets:** We recognize and accommodate specific dietary needs. This includes strict adherence to medical requirements (e.g., gluten intolerance, allergies with a certificate from allergologists or gastroenterologists) and personal dietary preferences (e.g., vegetarian, vegan). A minimum of a day each week is dedicated to vegetarian meals for the entire school community.

**Cultural and Social Aspects of Eating:** Meal times are integral to the Montessori "Practical Life" curriculum. Students actively participate in setting the table, serving food, and cleaning up afterward, learning valuable skills such as responsibility, community, and respect for their environment. Meals are shared within classrooms to foster a sense of community, making food a social experience that is nutritionally and emotionally enriching.

**Preparation with Care:** Our meals are lovingly prepared by our cooks, who bring passion and dedication to cooking. Their goal is to nourish the body and mind of each student and staff member through wholesome, carefully prepared meals.

**Feedback Mechanisms**

We collect monthly feedback from the Imagine team to review the menu for the following month and address any areas for improvement. All our menus are reviewed by a nutritional specialist to ensure they meet the highest standards of health and well-being.

## 4.4. Considerations for Fasting for Religious Reasons

Imagine Montessori School acknowledges and respects the cultural and religious diversity within our community. In alignment with our commitment to inclusivity and student well-being, the following measures are in place to support students fasting for religious reasons:

*   **Formal request** - Families who wish for their child to participate in fasting at school must submit a request to the Secretary. This request will initiate a Phidias process that must be reviewed and signed.
*   **Fasting Age Considerations** – While fasting is a personal and family decision, it is generally recommended for older children who can manage the physical and mental demands. Fasting during school hours is generally considered appropriate from puberty age, between 12 and 15 years old. Younger children who wish to participate should do so in a flexible manner, with shorter fasts or partial fasting, in consultation with their families.
*   **Provision of Quiet Spaces** – Fasting students will have access to a quiet space, free from food smells, such as the science lab, where they may read, engage in quiet conversation, or pray as needed. However, as Imagine Montessori School follows a classroom-based dining model without a common dining hall, the availability of such a space will depend on logistical possibilities within the school. Every effort will be made to provide a suitable environment that respects the needs of fasting students while maintaining the integrity of our Montessori approach to shared meal experiences.
*   **Respect for Fasting Students** – Staff and students are encouraged to be mindful of those fasting for religious reasons by refraining from eating or drinking in their presence.
*   **Health and Well-being Considerations** – While fasting is a personal and family decision, there may be circumstances in which a student requires food or water during the school day, such as feeling unwell, experiencing dizziness, due to menstruation or other health reasons.
    In cases where a student is visibly unwell or at risk of dehydration, the school reserves the right to provide immediate care, including offering water or food, if necessary, before contacting the parents. The well-being and safety of the child will always be the priority.
    Furthermore, if a fasting student requests food and/or water, it will be made available to them according to the school's standard procedures.
*   **Outdoor and Physical Activity Adjustments** – Fasting students may use outdoor spaces for socializing but should be mindful of their own energy levels and avoid excessive exposure to heat or strenuous activity. Parents are encouraged to have conversations with their children about these considerations to help them make informed choices regarding their activities while fasting.
*   **Afternoon Accommodations** – As fasting students may experience fatigue later in the day, if needed, we encourage them to find a quiet space to rest, read, or engage in low-energy activities as needed.
*   **Physical Education (PE) Modifications** – Participation in PE may be physically demanding while fasting. Students who require adjustments should be permitted to sit out or engage in alternative low-intensity activities, under the supervision of the same teacher.
*   **Respect for Personal and Family Decisions** – While the school provides accommodations for students who are fasting for religious reasons, it is considered a personal and family choice. The school does not monitor or enforce fasting, and students are responsible for managing their own participation in accordance with their family’s expectations.

    1.  When the facts reported are entirely implausible.

    4.  When the report contains no new and significant information regarding breaches in comparison with a previous report for which the procedures have been concluded, unless there are new factual or legal circumstances that justify a different follow-up. ## 6.9. Investigation

The investigation will encompass all actions aimed at verifying the credibility of the reported facts.

It will be ensured that the person affected by the information is informed of the report, as well as of the facts presented in a summarised form. Additionally, they will be informed of their right to submit written arguments and the handling of their personal data. However, this information may be provided at the hearing stage if prior disclosure could facilitate the concealment, destruction, or alteration of evidence.

In no case will the identity of the informant be disclosed to the affected parties, nor will they be given access to the report. During the investigation, a summary of the reported facts will be provided to the respondent. This information may also be presented at the hearing if prior disclosure could facilitate the concealment, destruction, or alteration of evidence.

Without prejudice to the right to submit written arguments, the investigation will include, whenever possible, an interview with the affected individual in which they are invited to present their version of the events and to provide any evidence they consider appropriate and relevant, all while respecting the presumption of innocence.

To ensure the right to a defence, the affected individual will have access to the case file without revealing any information that could identify the informant, and they may be heard at any point in the proceedings.

The System Administrator is obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the information they acquire during this investigation.

All Company personnel are required to cooperate with the investigation and must comply with any requests made to provide documentation, data, or any information related to the ongoing procedures, including personal data if required.

## 6.10. Conclusion of Proceedings

At the conclusion of the investigation, the System Administrator will adopt one of the following decisions:

a)  **Filing of the case**, which will be notified to the informant and, if applicable, to the affected individual. In such cases, the informant will retain the right to protection as provided by this law, unless, because of the actions carried out during the investigation phase, it is concluded that the report should have been dismissed based on one of the grounds specified in this Procedure.
b)  **Referral to the Public Prosecutor’s Office** if, despite initially finding no indication that the facts may constitute a crime, this determination changes over the course of the investigation. If the crime affects the financial interests of the European Union, the case will be referred to the European Public Prosecutor’s Office.
c)  **Transfer of all actions to the competent authority**, in accordance with the provisions of this Procedure.
d)  **Initiation of disciplinary proceedings**.

Upon conclusion of all actions, the System Administrator will retain information related to the report within the whistleblowing channel software, which will contain:

a)  Date of receipt.
b)  Classification of the report.
c)  Closure date.
d)  Actions taken.

This record will not be public, and only upon a reasoned request from the competent judicial authority, within the framework of a judicial proceeding and under its supervision, may partial or full access to the content of the record be granted.

Personal data related to received reports and internal investigations referred to in the preceding section will only be retained for as long as necessary and proportionate to comply with applicable legislation. In no case will data be retained for more than ten years.
