# Policy Compilation: Fire, Health, Weather, Food, Data, and Whistleblowing

This document compiles Imagine Montessori School's comprehensive policies across several critical operational and safety areas, ensuring a safe, healthy, and ethical environment for all children, staff, and visitors.

## 1. Fire and Emergency Policy

The [[Fire and Emergency Policy]] prioritizes the safety of children and staff through regular [[risk assessment]]s and termly emergency drills. [[Health and Safety Officers]] and an [[External Emergency Risk Technician]] are responsible for identifying hazards, evaluating risks, and implementing control measures. Key principles include continuous risk assessment, regular drills, clear communication for families and visitors, and accessible evacuation routes. Specific procedures cover staff absences (supply staff sweep), [[visitor management]] (registration, lanyards, host escort), and [[contractor management]] (recurrent vs. non-recurrent training and protocols). Evacuation routes are kept clear, and fire alarms and firefighting equipment are regularly tested and maintained. The [[Imagine Leadership Team]] reviews this policy biennially.

## 2. Health & Safety Policy

Imagine Montessori School's [[Health and Safety]] policy aims to support children's well-being by fostering risk recognition and applying proportionate safety measures. Ultimate responsibility rests with the employer, with specific tasks delegated to the [[HR Responsible]] for workplace safety and the [[Facility Manager]] for buildings and facilities. The [[Maintenance Team]] conducts daily checks on building conditions, exits, equipment, and lighting. All arrangements are monitored and reviewed annually, emphasizing equipment maintenance and hygiene. Staff receive comprehensive [[employee training]] on safety procedures, risk assessment, and [[first aid]]. A detailed protocol for [[wasp stings]] includes initial assessment, immediate action (especially for [[anaphylaxis]] and [[EpiPen]] use), and prevention strategies. The policy also covers [[contractor management]] selection, [[school security]], [[workplace stress]], and staff consultation. The [[Imagine Leadership Team]] reviews this policy biennially.

## 3. Policy of action in the event of weather alerts and emergencies

This policy outlines protocols for ensuring safety during [[weather alerts]] and emergencies, categorizing risks into Green (no risk), Yellow (moderate), Orange (significant), and Red (extreme). Measures escalate with risk level, from suspending outdoor activities at Yellow to considering early school closure or implementing [[confinement procedures]] at Orange, and activating evacuation/confinement plans at Red. [[Crisis management]] involves close coordination with the Generalitat Valenciana (GVA) and local authorities, whose directives on school closures are strictly followed. Preventive measures include regular checks of generators, drains, and first aid kits. The [[Head of the Emergency Plan]] is responsible for implementation and coordination, with biennial review by the [[Imagine Leadership Team]].

## 4. Food Policy

Imagine Montessori School's [[Food Policy]] emphasizes [[school nutrition]], [[sustainability]], and cultural aspects of eating, aligning with [[Montessori philosophy]]. It mandates three nutritious, balanced meals daily (snack, lunch, afternoon snack) using whole, unprocessed, low-salt, and sugar-free ingredients. A strong commitment to [[sustainable food practices]] means using organic, seasonal, and locally sourced ("km 0") products, with a weekly vegetarian meal day for the entire school community. Meal times are integrated into the "Practical Life" curriculum, fostering responsibility and community. The policy also accommodates [[special diets]] (medical or personal) and outlines a compassionate approach to students [[fasting for religious reasons]], providing quiet spaces and prioritizing health while respecting personal decisions. Menus are reviewed monthly with staff feedback and by a nutritional specialist. This policy is reviewed annually by the school leadership team.

## 5. Data Protection Privacy Policy

This [[Data Protection Policy]] details how Imagine Montessori School (ZUBI EDUCATION, S.L.) processes personal data in compliance with [[GDPR compliance]] (Regulation EU 2016/679) and Spain's LOPDGDD (Ley Orgánica 3/2018). Key principles include legality (explicit consent, contractual necessity, legitimate interest), data minimization, purpose limitation, accuracy, and robust [[data security]]. The policy specifies the types of personal data collected, methods of collection (e.g., Phidias, forms), and responsible entities (Data Controller, DPO). It outlines strict protocols for sharing data with third parties and international transfers, ensuring adequate protection. Users have comprehensive [[user rights]] (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, opposition, portability, consent withdrawal), with clear instructions on how to exercise them. The policy is reviewed biennially by the [[Imagine Leadership Team]].

## 6. Whistleblowing Channel Policy

This [[Whistleblowing Channel Policy]] establishes a secure and confidential system for employees, clients, suppliers, and third parties to report any breaches or irregularities, promoting [[ethical conduct]] without fear of retaliation. It aligns with EU Directive 2019/1937 and covers a broad range of reportable issues, including corruption, [[anti-money laundering]], [[data privacy]], [[cybersecurity]], [[equal opportunities]], harassment, and health & safety risks. The policy details the roles and responsibilities of the [[System Administrator]] and [[Investigation Committee]], ensuring reports are handled with confidentiality, respect for the [[presumption of innocence]], and adherence to data protection laws. Procedures include report receipt, registration, admission process (dismissal or admission), investigation, and conclusion, with clear timelines. Malicious or false claims are subject to disciplinary action. The [[Imagine Leadership Team]] reviews this policy biennially.
