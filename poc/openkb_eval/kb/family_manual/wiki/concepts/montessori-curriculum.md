# Concept: Montessori Curriculum

The [[Montessori curriculum]] is an educational philosophy and method developed by Dr. Maria Montessori, characterized by its child-centered approach, emphasis on independence, and use of a specially prepared environment with unique educational materials. It encourages self-directed learning, fostering a child's innate curiosity and promoting their holistic development—psychologically, physically, and emotionally.

## Core Principles and Implementation at Imagine Montessori School Valencia
At [[Imagine Montessori School Valencia]], the Montessori curriculum is integrated with the [[British National Curriculum of England and Wales]], creating a unique and comprehensive educational experience. Key aspects include:

*   **Prepared Environment**: Classrooms are organized with furniture and shelves designed to the students' height, making all materials completely accessible. This environment promotes autonomy and self-sufficiency.
*   **Self-Directed Learning**: Students are guided to work with materials and, once they've received a presentation from a Montessori guide, can continue to practice and repeat activities independently. This process allows them to acquire skills and achieve goals at their own pace.
*   **Sensory and Practical Materials**: The materials are sensory-based, facilitating practical, hands-on learning and aiding the transition from concrete to abstract concepts. They are specifically designed to teach, demonstrate, and allow students to practice concepts independently.
*   **Mixed-Age Groups**: Classrooms typically include students of three different ages, which promotes cooperation, reduces competition, and supports social development.
*   **Qualified Guides**: Teachers are qualified and experienced in using the Montessori method within a British curriculum environment, and the school supports their professional development.

## Alignment with the British National Curriculum
The Montessori philosophy and the UK educational curriculum are closely aligned, sharing common goals such as reinforcing children's natural curiosity and desire to learn, and helping them become inquisitive thinkers who consider diverse opinions. This synergy allows Imagine Montessori School Valencia to meet the objectives of the British curriculum while adhering to Montessori methodology.

## Montessori in Early Years and Primary

### First Years – Early Years
In the Infant Community and Children's House, the Montessori approach addresses the [[Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS)]] learning areas through the Montessori Work Cycle and integrated themes. Examples of activities include:

*   **Sensory Area**: Refines individual senses, indirectly preparing for language and mathematics.
*   **Cultural Area**: Explores botany, music, science, geography, zoology, history, and art through various materials and hands-on experiences.
*   **Practical Life**: Teaches daily tasks like serving food, cutting, and dressing, fostering independence and social grace.
*   **Language**: Develops from auditory discrimination to phonetic sounds (sandpaper letters), reading with Montessori materials, and various forms of writing, reinforced during 'Circle Time'.
*   **Mathematics**: Introduces numbers and operations using concrete materials like sandpaper numbers, number rods, beads, spindles, cubes, and counters, leading to visualization of the number system.

### Primary (6-12)
For Primary students, the curriculum continues to cover National Curriculum subjects within the Montessori work cycle. This stage harnesses the child's evolving conscious mind, strong interest in reasons, and desire for group work. Key elements include:

*   **[[Great Lessons (Montessori)]]**: Students are introduced to five "Great Lessons" (History of the Universe, History of Life on Earth, History of Humanity, Mathematics, Language) annually. These stories provide a framework for delving into traditional subjects with increasing depth.
*   **Abstraction**: The sequence of materials gradually leads students towards abstraction and paper-based work, building upon skills introduced in earlier stages.
*   **[[STEM education]]**: Content is enhanced through a cross-curricular "Creative Lab" workshop, integrating science, technology, engineering, and mathematics into hands-on projects.
