# School-Family Communication

Effective communication between the school and families is crucial for student well-being and parental peace of mind at [[Imagine Montessori School Valencia]]. The school employs a variety of platforms and channels to ensure smooth and dynamic information exchange.

## Communication Platforms and Channels
As outlined in [[summaries/family_manual]], the primary communication tools include:

*   **Phidias**: This is the main communication tool for interactions between the pedagogical team, administration, and families. It provides access to:
    *   Relevant school documents (monthly menu, photos, school calendar, yearbooks).
    *   Personal user space for payments.
    *   Processes for `pick-up authorization` and `Imagine Shop` purchases.
    *   Grading and reporting for secondary students.
    *   Authorization for medication administration (`How to authorize the administration of medication through Phidias` link in the source).
    *   General inquiries to administration (e.g., about payments).
    *   Updating personal information or family situation (`How to edit your profile on Phidias` link in the source).
    *   Authorizing student pick-up or independent departure (`How to Authorise Student Pick-Up in Phidias` link in the source).
    *   Outing authorizations (`How to complete an Outing Authorization` link in the source).
*   **Transparent Classroom**: Used for the Infant Community, Children’s House, and Primary sections, this is a **one-way pedagogical communication tool**. Teaching staff share observations about students and weekly activities with families.
*   **Individual Meetings with Families**: Held at the end of the first and third terms, these meetings allow Guides to discuss student progress with families, supported by a written report sent via Transparent Classroom or Phidias.
*   **Newsletter**: A periodic publication sharing relevant school community events.
*   **Daily Communication**: Guides in the Infant Community and Children’s House are available during drop-off and pick-up times for informal discussions. For Primary and Secondary, daily communication with Guides is primarily via Phidias.
*   **Tutorials**: Each Guide has designated weekly tutorial hours for addressing specific issues, which can be requested via Phidias.
*   **Pedagogical Meetings**: Quarterly meetings are organized between families and educators to discuss educational guidelines and the school’s pedagogical approach. These are offered both in-person and online, in separate sessions for each language.

## Language of Communication

*   **Administrative and secretarial matters**: Communicated in both Spanish and English.
*   **Pedagogical matters**: Primarily in English, with a link provided for Spanish translation. This allows teaching staff to prioritize direct engagement with students while ensuring Spanish-speaking families can understand the content.

## Specific Communication Procedures

*   **Contacting Personnel**: Queries about pedagogical content and personal child matters should go to the child's Guide. Administrative or operational inquiries are directed to specific leadership and administration roles (e.g., Executive Director for school policies, Head Teacher for pedagogical topics, Head of School Operations for enrichment, Administration for payments, Secretaries for logistics).
*   **Reporting Absences or Changes**: Any student absences or changes in arrival/departure times (e.g., for medical appointments) must be reported in advance via a Phidias message to both the administration and the student’s Guide/tutor, or by calling the school for unforeseen circumstances.
*   **Medication Administration**: Requires a specific Phidias tracking form to be completed and signed by families.
*   **Bus Service Changes**: Must be communicated before 2:30 p.m. via a designated form, not by phone or email.
*   **Feedback and Support**: The school encourages families to reach out via Phidias for any questions regarding payments or financial aid. If issues like frequent late arrivals or behavioral incidents arise, the school initiates meetings with families to assess and support the situation.
