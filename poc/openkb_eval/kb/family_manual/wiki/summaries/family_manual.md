# Family Manual: Imagine Montessori School Valencia 2024/25

This handbook serves as a comprehensive guide for families joining or continuing with [[Imagine Montessori School Valencia]] for the 2024/25 school year. It outlines the school's structure, pedagogical approach, daily operations, and key policies.

## School Overview
Imagine Montessori School Valencia is a private, co-educational, and secular institution combining the [[Montessori Curriculum]] with the [[British National Curriculum]] (England and Wales). It is recognized and authorized by both the UK Department for Education and the Spanish Ministry of Education, emphasizing a creative, hands-on approach and fostering student autonomy, curiosity, and community belonging.

## Key Personnel & Communication
The school provides clear contact points for pedagogical and administrative matters, encouraging communication via Phidias. Pedagogical queries are directed to the child's Guide, while administrative needs are handled by the Administration team. [[School-Family Communication]] also utilizes Transparent Classroom (one-way pedagogical updates), individual meetings, newsletters, and designated tutorial hours.

## Timetables and Logistics
Timetables vary by age group (Infant Community, Children’s House, Primary, Secondary) with options for Morning Session, Reduced Day, and Full Day. Punctuality is highly valued, with specific arrival (8:45-9:00 a.m.) and departure (4:40-4:55 p.m.) guidelines. Late arrivals and pick-ups incur fees to encourage adherence and minimize disruption. Dedicated [[Punctuality Guidelines]] are in place.

Specific logistics are provided for drop-off and pick-up, including separate entrances for the Infant Community. Additional services include "Early Bird" (8:00-8:45 am) and "Afternoon Club" (4:40-6:00 pm). Authorization for student pick-up or independent departure requires updating records in Phidias.

## Curriculum and Pedagogy
The school integrates the [[Montessori Curriculum]] with the [[British National Curriculum]].

### Early Years (Infant Community & Children's House)
Focuses on the three core areas of the [[Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS)]]: Personal, Social and Emotional Development (PSED), Physical Development (PD), and Communication and Language (C&L), before moving to specific areas like Literacy and Mathematics. Activities are rooted in Montessori's sensory, practical life, language, mathematics, and cultural areas.

### Primary (6-12)
Students engage with National Curriculum subjects within the Montessori work cycle, moving from absorbent to a more conscious mind. The curriculum is structured around Maria Montessori's "[[Great Lessons (Montessori)]]" which include the History of the Universe, Life on Earth, Humanity, Mathematics, and Language. [[STEM Education]] content is enhanced through a "Creative Lab" workshop.

## Support Departments
*   **[[Psycho Pedagogical Guidance]]**: Identifies special educational needs, advises families on external specialists, and coordinates adjustments and action plans. It does not conduct diagnoses or therapy but provides continuous support.
*   **[[Career Guidance]]**: Offers vocational counselling, information on educational/career options, employability skill development, and liaison with businesses and professionals.

## Nutrition and Health
The school prioritizes healthy eating with daily-made, organic, seasonal, and locally-sourced meals, free from processed ingredients. An educational approach involves students in gardening and composting. After meals, students brush their teeth. [[Healthy Eating Policy]] details are provided.

[[School Health Policy]] mandates that ill students do not attend school (e.g., fever, vomiting, diarrhea within 24 hours). Medications are only administered with a medical prescription and written parental authorization via Phidias. Families must provide a medical certificate confirming adherence to the Valencian Community's vaccination schedule at specified ages.

## Student Life & Practicalities
*   **Behavior**: Incidents are documented, and families are informed. Repeated incidents lead to meetings with teachers and the educational psychology team.
*   **English Language Commitment**: As a British school, English is the language of instruction, and families are encouraged to support English language development at home.
*   **[[School Dress Code]]**: No uniform (except for PE and excursions), but comfortable, practical, and labelled clothing is required. Spare clothing is mandatory for Early Years and Primary.
*   **Nappies and Accidents**: Families provide nappies/wipes for Early Years. Children's House expects toilet-trained students. Spare clothes are essential for accidents.
*   **Birthday Celebrations**: Focus on non-food activities (e.g., decorating, bringing a book), with a no-foods policy due to allergies.
*   **Enrichment Programme**: Extracurricular activities designed to foster creativity and social skills.
*   **School Materials/Toys/Balls**: School materials taken home must be returned. Toys are not permitted (except naptime comfort items). Balls are allowed outdoors but must be contained for bus users.
*   **Lost and Found**: Unclaimed, unlabelled items are cleared monthly after a display session.
*   **Outings**: Complement curriculum, may incur extra costs, and require specific Phidias consent. Students must travel with the school group.
*   **School Bus**: Three routes available, contracted annually. Changes require advance notification via form.
*   **Personal Information**: Families must update contact and family situation changes via Phidias.
*   **Photographs**: Families provide consent for internal photo sharing. Specific authorization is needed for media use. An annual yearbook with professional photos is available digitally for an additional cost.

## Financial Information
[[School Financial Aid]] includes Valencia/Paterna City Council vouchers, Sodexo nursery vouchers, and tax deductions for working mothers. Some government aids are not applicable to private British curriculum schools like Imagine. Fees are charged monthly via direct debit. Enrollment fees for the following course are refunded if non-continuity is communicated before February 1st.
