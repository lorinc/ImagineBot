# Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is a fundamental principle emphasizing honesty, trust, fairness, respect, and responsibility in all academic pursuits. It means that all work submitted by a student is their own original effort, without unauthorized assistance or misrepresentation.

At Imagine Montessori School, as outlined in [[summaries/policy5]], academic integrity is a core expectation, particularly highlighted in the External Examinations Policies section. The school's "Internal Appeals Procedure & Assessment Reviews" document explicitly defines academic integrity as "The principle of being honest and responsible in academic work, ensuring all work submitted is genuinely one’s own."

## Key Aspects and Expectations

Students at Imagine Montessori School receive clear guidance on maintaining academic integrity, which includes understanding and avoiding:

*   **[[concepts/plagiarism|Plagiarism]]**: Presenting someone else’s work or ideas as one’s own without proper acknowledgement.
*   **Collusion**: Unauthorized collaboration with others on work that is meant to be completed individually.
*   **Academic Misconduct**: Any action that gives a student an unfair academic advantage or misrepresents their own work.
*   **Misuse of [[concepts/ai_in_education|AI Tools]]**: The school has a specific policy regarding the use of artificial intelligence tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Grammarly):
    *   AI tools *may* be used for planning, brainstorming, and revision, but only if permitted by the teacher.
    *   They *must not* be used to generate or complete assessable work unless explicitly authorized.
    *   Any use of AI tools as a source in a submission *must* be acknowledged.

## Consequences of Breaches

Breaches of academic integrity, including [[concepts/exam_malpractice|exam malpractice]] or plagiarism, are taken very seriously. As detailed in [[summaries/policy5]], consequences may include:

*   Referral to an Academic Misconduct Panel or Head of Year.
*   Possible disqualification from the assessment or disciplinary action, depending on the severity of the offense.
*   In cases involving external assessments, exam boards may be notified, leading to more severe penalties such as loss of marks, disqualification from a subject, or disqualification from all qualifications for an exam series.
