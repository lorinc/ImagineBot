# Summary of "policy5" - Imagine Montessori School Policies

This document outlines Imagine Montessori School's comprehensive policies across several critical areas, reflecting its commitment to a safe, inclusive, and educationally rich environment. Rooted in the Montessori philosophy, the school emphasizes grace, courtesy, autonomy, and curiosity.

## 1. Our Commitment & Equal Opportunities
The school is dedicated to fostering core values such as respect for [[cultural diversity]], individual worth, fairness, and mutual support. It actively promotes these values through Personal, Social, Emotional (PSE) and Personal, Social, Health and Economic (PSHE) lessons and assemblies, specifically addressing [[stereotypes]] and [[discrimination]]. The school is committed to [[equal opportunities]] for all, adhering to the Equality Act 2010 and ensuring [[anti-discriminatory practices]] across all grounds (gender, age, race, religion, disability, etc.). Objectives include supporting [[ethnic minority groups]], students with [[disabilities]] or [[learning difficulties]], eliminating [[racism]] and [[sexism]], and preparing students for a multicultural society.

## 2. Dress Code Policy
While promoting [[cultural diversity]] and individual expression by not enforcing a uniform, the policy sets gender-neutral [[dress code]] restrictions for safety and focus. Key principles are inclusivity, safety, practicality, modesty, and clarity. Specific guidelines cover headwear (allowing [[religious headwear]]), footwear, general everyday attire, and PE day wear, as well as a distinct dress code for staff.

## 3. Attendance Policy
The school stresses the importance of [[attendance]] and [[punctuality]] for student learning and development. It defines [[authorised absence]] and [[unauthorised absence]], outlines responsibilities for staff and parents, and details procedures for registration, dealing with lateness, and addressing [[persistent absence]]. Target attendance rates are set (80% for CH, 90% for Primary, 95% for Secondary), with a focus on early intervention and parental partnership.

## 4. Behaviour Policy
Imagine Montessori cultivates a positive and respectful environment through its [[Positive Behaviour Approach]], where staff act as [[role models]]. The policy emphasizes [[grace and courtesy]], respect for [[cultural differences]], and individual needs. [[Natural consequences]] are preferred over extrinsic motivation. A structured [[Behaviour Flowchart]] guides responses to minor and major incidents, involving a [[Student Support Team]] (SST) and parents. A 3-tier system addresses escalating behavioural issues, with Tier 3 potentially requiring individual support plans and one-to-one assistance. Physical restraint is only used in extreme safety situations and must be documented. The policy includes strategies for managing [[disagreements between pupils]] (e.g., [[Peace Table]], circle time) and addressing [[inappropriate language]] and [[rough play]].

## 5. Anti-Racism Policy
Recognizing its multi-ethnic and international community, the school is proactive in promoting [[racial equality]] and eliminating [[racial discrimination]]. It defines [[racism]] as both [[institutional racism]] (collective failure, unwitting prejudice) and [[personal racism]] (direct harassment like physical assault, derogatory name-calling, racist graffiti). The policy outlines responsibilities for the Senior Leadership Team and all staff to challenge racism, investigate incidents, and apply disciplinary actions, including potential police involvement.

## 6. Anti-Bullying Policy
The school is strongly committed to preventing [[bullying]] in all forms (physical, verbal, psychological, [[cyber-bullying]], prejudice-based). It aims to create a safe and supportive environment where students feel confident to report bullying. The policy distinguishes bullying from normal disagreements and details prevention strategies (e.g., PSHEE, social stories, role-plays), procedures for identifying and reporting incidents, and actions/consequences including formal warnings, [[suspension]], and [[exclusion]].

## 7. Drug and Alcohol Policy
This policy covers all legal and illegal substances, including alcohol, tobacco, vape pens, and New Psychoactive Substances (NPS), applying to all community members on and off-site. It highlights the school’s zero-tolerance stance on possession, use, and sale of drugs, with exceptions for prescribed medicines. [[Drugs education]] is integrated into the PSHE program, covering risks, legal implications, and healthy choices. Procedures for managing incidents, involving parents, and cooperating with police and emergency services are detailed, alongside staff conduct guidelines.

## 8. Weapons and Dangerous Items Policy
The policy maintains a zero-tolerance approach to [[weapons]] and [[dangerous items]] on school premises, defining various prohibited items from firearms and bladed objects to tools and explosives. It emphasizes a [[preventative culture]] through safeguarding and PSHE education. Procedures include searching (with or without consent, with two staff members), confiscation, and deciding on next actions based on risk and intent, potentially leading to exclusion or the implementation of a [[safety plan]] upon return.

## 9. External Examinations Policies
### 9.1. Malpractice Policy
Addresses [[exam malpractice]] for GCSE and A-Level exams, defining acts like using unauthorised materials, copying, [[collusion]], [[plagiarism]], or unpermitted [[AI tools]]. Procedures involve investigation, evidence collection, reporting to exam boards, and sanctions up to disqualification.

### 9.2. Internal Appeals & Assessment Reviews
Outlines procedures for appealing internal assessment decisions (coursework, NEAs) and requesting Enquiries About Results (EARs) for external exams. It reinforces [[academic integrity]] and provides a clear [[AI Usage Policy]], specifying acceptable and unacceptable uses of AI tools and consequences for misuse.
